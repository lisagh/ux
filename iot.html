who are caregivers

There are different types of caregivers in America and varies state by state. They include certificated/non-certificated caregivers, nurses, therapists, social workers and more. In this case, we would focus on caregivers who provide the skilled-care for aging people.





The Gap

According to the report, Caregiving Innovation Frontiers of AARP( American Association of Retired Persons), it shows, by 2020, 117 million of Americans are expected to need the assistance of some kind, but the unpaid caregivers are only expected to reach 45 million.

GOAL

A senior-user-friendly product helps to get a seamless connection between the elderly who took joint replacement surgery and their caregivers/families.

Solution: SmartCare

Smart Care aims to provide the best and easiest user experiences for both elderly users and their caregivers. This product includes a smartwatch and a pair mobile application supported by IBM Watson cloud services.


user scenario



Design Process

research

Hip and knee replacements are on the rise in recent years. A report from HealthinAging.org says nearly 700,000 Americans have hip or knee joint replacement surgery every year.
Based on the American Joint Replacement Registry 2017 Annual Report, the senior group has the highest portion compared with younger generations.



Expert Feedback

We received the feedback from the homecare experts: Leslie A Myers, from IU Health Home Care, and Anna M. Stanley, from Indiana University North Hospital after we presented the second-stage product plan in this design challenge.

"The hospital that performs the surgery only gets paid if the patient does not return in 60 days. What they do now is have a nurse visit the patient at their house to make sure they are exercising – doing PHYSICAL THERAPY. Your product and service could be an Automated Physical Therapy system for elderly joint replacement market."

Customer Profile

Pain Points


  • An efficient connection between the patient and his/her caregivers

  • Track patient status smart and guarantee an on-time help

  • Poor post-operation physical therapy management

Brainstorm

After collecting all the information that we need, we executed a brainstorming workshop to get the ideas. There are different possibilities such as Xbox, motion tracker, healthcare scanner, smart glasses, smart slipper, and smart necklace. At the end of the ideation phase, we decided to go with the smartwatch and mobile app.

Conceptual Design

System Model

In this concept, there are five main functions:

  • the smartwatch can detect several body conditions and upload the data to the server.

  • the system can deliver the physical therapy plan from the caregiver/therapist to the patient.

  • the caregiver can check the progress or get emergency alert of the patient in his or her devices.

  • considering the elderly memory recession, it would be useful to have a voice control widget as a reminder to tell the elderly when to exercise or do other things.

  • the IBM AI service can be served as the analyzer to generate the report and discover potential trend or change for the elderly patient.




PROTOTYPES

We made a lo-fi prototype for both the smartwatch and the mobile app to present our idea. On the watch side, we applied the large icons and less text on the screen for aging people. And for the mobile app, the collected data would be visualized into different charts.



Design Decision

Why monitoring

It is hard to ignore the truth that aging people are facing more health issues than younger folks. The monitoring technology would be one of the solution to help your parents reduced the risk when they live alone. Even for healthcare providers, like hospitals or nursing homes, seamless connections with senior patients would give powerful support when diagnosing.

Why smartwatch?

Smartwatch is easy to carry on. It is multifunctional as well. Even for the financial consideration, the smartwatch is the best option for the healthcare monitoring products in the wearable devices market.
