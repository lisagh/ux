Hi, welcome to Newfields!

Overview

As the ninth oldest and eighth largest encyclopedic art museum in the United States, Newfields has over 54,000 works, including African, American, Asian, and European pieces.

problem

Current paper maps/directional signage was the top issue for visitors who easily lost their way in the museum.

Design Process



Research

Business Analysis

The wayfinding app is a good applied example that helps people find their way. A common technology for indoor use is Indoor Positioning Systems (IPS).

Two commercial products that utilize IPS for wayfinding are Wifarer and Navibees. Wifarer for museums gives guests access to location-aware content, which lets them know about nearby exhibits and allows them to drill down into the information. It also provides guests with real-time interior positioning utilizing the museum's existing WiFi or Bluetooth beacons. The museum can manage the content through an integrated Content Management System (CMS) and view analytics to see which exhibits are popular and where guests are spending their time.

Navibees for museums provides many of the same features as Wifarer. The additional capabilities include creating special routes that guests can use to tour the museum and allows guests to share their location via social media integration.

Style Guide

Design Decision

Why ask?

In this screen the guest is asked to indicate what they want to do at the museum by selecting either play, learn, or relax.
This question was created based on feedback from the Newfields Guest Services department. The goal was to create an interaction with the application that mimics a conversation a guest might have with one of Newfields staff when they ask, “What is cool here?”

Why map and list?

The map with the list style borrows from the Yelp app. We only designed the map view for the users to explore the museum in the beginning. When doing the design pitch in a meeting at Newfields, an engineer from the IT department responded that some users would prefer the list view rather than the map look. After discussion, we iterated the design with the map-list method.

Why the detail page?

Based on the research, the museum guests were eager to access more exhibit information while visiting. The detail page integrated the art piece information, related artworks, audio player, and also the marker function.

EVALUATION

To perform usability testing of the design, we built the prototype as an Android application. The app works with the Estimote Proximity Beacons which developed with Android Studio and runs on a Samsung Galaxy S8 phone.





Method

Participants were asked to complete five different tasks:

  • indicating initial preferences

  • removing a marker from the map view

  • searching for an exhibit

  • navigating to that exhibit

  • viewing the rest floor maps of the museum

At the end of each task, participants were asked to rate the difficulty of the task on a 5-point scale. (1 = very difficult, 5 = very easy)





Participants




Result

The mean SUS score was 87.5 (range 80 – 100), which is above the “industry average” score of 68 (Sauro, 2011). This score suggests that generally, the participants found the prototype to be usable.
