EXPLORING IUPUI

On the eve of IUPUI's 50th anniversary, visit the campus in a 3D/VR world and interact with its history online. I was responsible for UX/UI/data design and development in this project. Live project here

The Exploring IUPUI project is supported by the IUPUI Welcoming Campus Innovation program and the Indiana University Bicentennial Project. The goal is to encourage users to explore the legacy and heritage of the IUPUI campus and to better understand the history of the University.

How people explore the campus?

There are some ways for people to know more about the campus. Option one, schedule a visit in person. Option two, google the campus map and see the street view. Option three, visit the interactive IUPUI campus map which provides more details including the name of the building and what academic units are located there.

Exploring IUPUI can do more

Virtual Reality
As one of the most popular technologies in the world, VR provides users with enhance user experience. The 3D/VR team in this project re-created the current campus as well as "resurrected" demolish buildings.

Campus History Tour
Although VR is fun, engaging, and edifying, the web product could access to more users. An online campus history tour allows the user to interact with various media elements and get connected to the history.

But how?

While the 3D designers modeled the virtual world, the UX designers delivered an online campus history tour.

Design Requirments

After having several meetings with the PIs, history team, 3D team and my colleagues, I summarized these key design elements of the project:

1. To design a website for users who want to know more about the IUPUI campus that is easy to access and interact with.
2. To integrate 3D and VR into the web tool.

The Solution

The Exploring IUPUI landing page provides the virtual reality download resource, Web 3D tour powered by WebGL, and the building history pages.

Data Design
The data here represents information related to the campus including when buildings were erected, if they had a former name and various stories related to those buildings. This data was collected by history team and is cited whenever possible.

Reflection
