The Challenge

Design a service that empowers older adults to help them achieve personal freedom while respecting their digital dignity.

Created a "Newco", Squarer, which provides a home-based physical therapy IoT connection to the targeting users: the elderly patients and their caregivers.

Design Process

Empathise

1. Research

Summary
In this project, we focus on the home-based eldercare market. After doing some research and using the four teardrops to analyzed the current situation, we picked a company as a company persona to continue helping us to gain an empathic understanding of this topic.
The Market
According to the report, Caregiving Innovation Frontiers, of AARP( American Association of Retired Persons), it shows by 2020, 117 million of Americans are expected to need the assistance of some kind but the unpaid caregivers are only expected to reach 45 million.

Business Model Environment of the Home-based Eldercare Market

Industry Forces
  1. Moderate power of supplier
  2. Low buyer power(relationship with the certain homecare provider)
  3. Low costs of market entry and exit for the homecare providers rivalry is region dependent
  4. Hospitalization and nursing homecare are two current substitutes
Market Forces
  1. Aging population is increasing
  2. Hard to shift the health care practice to patient’s home
  3. Insurance reimbursements versus cost
Key Trend
  1. Technology-driven products can expand the services or improve the current solutions
  2. The gap between assistance needed population and the number of unpaid caregivers
  3. The growth of online home delivery
  4. Homecare workers get better pay
Macroeconomics
  1. The rising cost of healthcare in the U.S.
  2. Medicare, Medicaid to remain top sources of coverage, reimbursements
  3. Global aging trend keep spurring the overseas aging related business

4. Pain Points

After understanding the market and know who are the users, we summarized the pain points based on what we got.

  • How to connect the patient and the caregivers?
  • How to track the patient's status?
  • What's the way to manage the post-operation physical therapy at home?

Ideate

1. Brainstorm

Usually, after the patient received a knee replacement, the orthopedic surgeon and physical therapist will suggest him or her exercise for 20 to 30 mins, 2 to 3 times a day and walk around 30 mins as well. In order to have a safe recovery, it's necessary to check with the therapist or surgeon before performing any types of exercise.
After collecting all information that we need, as a team, we executed a brainstorming workshop to get the ideas that help to solve our problem. We explored different possibilities such as Xbox, motion tracker, healthcare scanner, smart glasses, smart slipper, and smart necklace. At the end of the ideation phase, we decided to go with the motion tracker and mobile app.

2. Conceptual Design

System Model
We tried to design an IoT wearable device for the elderly to track their daily and exercise motion which is easy to take and use. In this concept, there are three main functions:
1. the wearable device can detect several body conditions and upload the data to the server.
2. the system can deliver the physical therapy plan from the caregiver/therapist to the patient.
3. the caregiver can check the progress or get emergency alert of the patient in his or her devices.
4. considering the elderly memory recession, it would be useful to have a voice control widget as a reminder to tell the elderly when to exercise or do other things.
5. the IBM AI service can be served as the analyzer to generate the report and discover potential trend or change for the elderly patient.

Emergency care
We noticed that, if the elderly live alone and the emergency happened during the post-operation period, it's hard to get help from the caregivers or families in time. For example, when the patient doing physical therapy but accidentally fall down, how do the IoT device can help?

We wrapped the idea and made a 20 mins pitch to Susann Keohane, from IBM Research and Adam Culter, from IBM Design. They were very interested in the combination of the eldercare and physical exercise and gave some feedback like below:

  • Walked right up to the edge of a huge opportunity space
  • The integration of biometric sensors with a back-end AI engine customizing exercise routines was extremely interesting and potentially innovative, but you did not take full advantage of the possibilities of that system could imagine.

3. Design Iteration

After reviewing the feedback, we reconsidered the design concept and refactored the system.
Currently what we have, is focusing on the data management to provide a workflow for the user, once the user takes the wearable device, he or she would automatically get connected to the caregivers with no worries.
However, from the user's perspective, we also need to consider the scenario of the physical therapy. So, we provided two new design to improve the user experience.
1. Xbox Fitness, track body movement
We borrowed the movement tracking idea from Xbox Fitness, in this environment, the sensors are kept monitoring the body move and giving out the voice feedback all the time. The video below shows how the function works


2. Can I do some exercise now?
Some of the elderly patients may not know when to do the physical exercise is better. Exercising at the worst times can be hazardous to the health. In this case, when the device tracks the body data, it can evaluate the current status and know whether is a good time to do exercise or not.
3. Updated System Map
For the elderly patient, the core of the system can provide the information like when to do the physical activities and how to do it correctly. We drew a circle to simulate the workflow. See Figure 8.

Firstly, the caregivers and therapists can generate the training plans within the system based on the patient's postoperative performance. The patient can start the training plan at home by him or herself. while the patient is wearing the wristband, the system can collect the vital signs like BPM in real time.
Secondly, we added a Kinect-like device which can track the motion and provide voice feedback, like the Xbox Fitness. This function can support the patient better and safer when they are doing the exercise.
Thirdly, all the collected data would be uploaded to the server and then generate the reports which can deliver to both the patient and the caregivers.
Last but not least, the caregivers can easily follow up the patients' postoperative performances and review, update the training plans according to the needs.

Future Work

As a Newco project, it targeted a specific customer segment and focused on how to make up the gap between the elderly patient and the caregivers. Not just doing the design stuff, we also established the basic business model based on the current market situation. After optimizing the system design, the next step would be focusing on the user experience design with more functions and screens that can give a hint to our potential customers.

Lesson Learned

1. It's important to find the right customer segment.
2. Try to solve a small problem, not a broad problem.
3. Need to be a strategic designer, a real-world product always have more business considerations.
4. Design iteration not just makes the design better, but makes the designer better.
5. Refactoring this project is a tough work. But I made it.
